Overview

  • Have looked at how to help novices, how to fix exercises to human brains, and some different kinds of exercises
  • Next question: how to keep people motivated?
  • "It is not enough to preach–one must be heard."

Effective Teachers

  • Know the subject material well
    • But not necessarily to expert level
  • Ask questions rather than giving directions
    • Constructivism
  • Ignore trivial errors and focus on significant ones
    • Our Content Developers will help you write exercise tests to do this
  • Tell a story
    • Human beings are narrative animals

Adult Learners

  1. Value self-efficacy
    • Accept responsibility for making progress
  2. Consciously draw on previous knowledge
    • Will question instructors based on prior knowledge rather than simply rebel
  3. Pragmatic: want knowledge they can use
    • Make their own judgments about whether knowledge is useful

Self-Efficacy

  • Macro: they decide what to do and when to do it
  • Micro: they decide when to get help and when not to
  • Important to accept alternate valid solutions to exercises
    • "You have to do it my way" takes control away from them
    • But checking all possible alternatives is hard

Prior Knowledge

  • Facilitate recall by giving context for references
    • "The previous chapter showed you how to load CSV data…"
  • Present new knowledge as extension or alternative to existing knowledge
    • "Here's another way to load the same data…"
  • Require learners to use what they already know to check their answers
    • "Sure enough, the total cost is zero."

Pragmatism

  • Use authentic tasks
    • If your variables are called foo and bar, learners have to do extra work to construct meaning
  • Demonstrate utility early to build learners' confidence in the value of what you're teaching
    • What's your minimum viable example?

Pragmatism

Vary Stimulus

  • Predictability quickly becomes boring
    • But meaningless variation IS distracting
  • Alternate different kinds of exercises or goals
  • Alternate different kinds of goals
    • Learning a method vs. solving a problem

Text and Graphics

  • Mix text with graphics
  • But do not duplicate information
    • Correlating parallel inputs consumes mental processing power
  • Use diagrams in exercises
    • "Which of these labels should be attached at X, Y, and Z in the diagram?"
    • "Which of these three fragments of code produces the data structure shown?"

Major Demotivators

  • Unpredictability
    • If cause does not lead to effect, people stop trying
  • Indifference
    • Learners will never care more about the subject than you do
    • Or than you care about them
  • Unfairness
    • If a system is unfair, people invest less in it
    • Even if it's unfair in their favor

Demotivation in Practice

  • A holier-than-thou attitude
    • "I'll speak slowly for the Excel users."
  • Expert blind spot
    • "Obviously, it's just duck typing within a closure."
  • Opaque references
    • "Let's see if we can pull a Billy Smith from the lonely end of the rink."
  • Exclusionary language
    • "So simple that even your mother could do it."
