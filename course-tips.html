Overview

  • Even with good will on all sides, things will go wrong
  • Some problems come up more often than others, so we have advice on how to avoid them
  • And a process for addressing them when they haven't been avoided

The Audience

  • You know your content
  • We know our audience
  • If we say that an approach isn't going to work for our users, trust us
    • We won't try to make extra work for anyone

Too Much Math

  • We want to teach people how to use code to apply math to real-world problems
  • Deriving equations and proving theorems is out of scope
    • And not a fit for our platform
  • This isn't dumbing things down
  • It's providing an on-ramp to as wide an audience as possible
    • Soup, then hymns

Too Much Code

  • Never more than a few lines at a time
    • Remember 7±2
  • Avoid esoteric language features
    • Python for loops can have an else clause
    • Most people don't know this
    • Those who do usually misremember how it works

Not Enough of Either

  • Our users aren't afraid of math or code
  • They're just primarily interested in their application
  • Work through at least one course similar to yours before starting to write
    • Good musicians listen to each other
    • CLs and CDs can recommend ones that are highly rated

Preaching to Believers

  • Our users already believe that data science is fun
  • They already know it's changing the world
  • So you don't need to tell them this

Teasers

  • Don't say "it's really important to think about X"
  • Their response will (rightly) be, "Think what about X?"
  • Similarly, "Make this web site more webbish, but not too webbish" never helped anyone
    • If you can't provide rules, provide examples
    • Like the Python for-else example above

Writer's Block

  • The first page is the hardest
    • Except for the second
    • And the third…
  • Our course design process is meant to get you past the worst of this
  • When in doubt, talk to your CL

Procrastination

  • We can't write the course for you
  • Weekly check-ins are meant to keep you moving
  • If it becomes clear that content is never going to materialize, it's better to part ways amicably than drag things out

Perfectionism

  • No course will ever be right for everyone
  • We will provide guidance and feedback as you work
    • Pace and clarity
    • Voice and grammar
    • How engaging examples are
  • Soft launch always catches things that authors and CDs didn't expect
    • "No lesson survives first contact with the real world intact."

Platform Issues

  • We're constantly trying to improve our tools
  • Which means that occasionally things don't work as we'd like
  • Please tell us when anything seems off
    • If it's a bug, we'll fix it
    • If we can improve the user experience for instructors, we'll add it to our backlog

What We Just Did

  1. Enumerated some common pitfalls
  2. Looked at ways we can address them together
