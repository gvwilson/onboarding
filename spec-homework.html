Overview

  • Four short exercises to reinforce what you have just learned and spark discussion
  • Please do before coming to the live discussion
  • Share by posting your answers to the shared Google Doc (we'll send you the link)

1. Stumbling Blocks

Think about the language features and libraries your learners will use, and the terminology they will encounter. What do you think will be most confusing or hardest to justify? (For example, it's often hard to explain to newcomers why Python has both lists and tuples.)

2. Multiple Choice Question

Create a multiple-choice question that tests understanding of one of the concepts you intend to cover in your course. Include the correct answer and 2-3 incorrect answers, and explain what misconception each incorrect answer is intended to diagnose.

3. Debugging Exercise

Write a brief description of a debugging exercise you might have learners do in your course. Include 2-3 sentences explaining what the code is supposed to do and 5-10 lines of code that almost do that.

4. Personal Motivation

Create a point-form summary of what you hope to get out of developing a course for DataCamp.
